UNDULATING HORIZONS

- An Ecological Institute on Newtown Creek with Water-Inspired Form

Considerations of System, Ecology, and Urban Context


Starting from exploring the materiality of water, the class was asked to develop a conceptual/ abstract system that could self-iterate to create the spatial prototype which later on fit the programs of an ecological institute that provide both a labatory space that monitor the Newtown Creek ecological index and public spaces including two daylit galleries and an auditorium.


In my case, by exploring the recreation of the imagery of the water in which inhabitable spaces form by boundaries of undulating surfaces, I experimented with different sptial prototypes...

Housing No. 17, Missing Middle

1 lot. 2 row houses. Adaptable to multiple lots. A lot. A larger lot. Another lot. Modular Construction. Ceramic panels on a lightweight structural frame. Everything arrives on trucks. Stacked. The roof collects rainwater and solar. Terraces, front and back.
